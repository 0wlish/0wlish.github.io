Paleoart!

Archaeopteryx

Gouache on paper of Archaeopteryx, widely regarded as "the first bird." 2023.

Anchiornis

Acrylic on canvas board depicting Anchiornis, a genus of paravian dinosaurs from the Late Jurassic, feasting on a small lizard among the Claytosmunda ferns. Williamsonia cycads and Ginkgo trees overlook the scene. The coloration of Anchiornis is based off of an examination of the dinosaur's melanosomes (source: https://doi.org/10.1086/721620). The depiction of the foliage in the piece was inspired by Henri Rousseau's work. 2024.

Sinosauropteryx with Ephedra

Acrylic on canvas showing a thicket of Ephedra shrubs from the arid Cretaceous-era Jehol Biota. Sinosauropteryx, a genus of compsognathid dinosaurs, creeps through the undergrowth. I based the patterning off of an analysis of fossil with particularly well-preserved feathers; the analysis hypothesized that the dinosaur's coloring allowed it to camouflage itself in open areas with sparse cover (source: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cub.2017.09.032). 2024.

Tiktaalik

Ceramic figurine of the animal of the same name. Tiktaalik is a transition fossil between fish and terrestrial tetrapods and may be the common ancestor of all land-dwelling vertebrate life. Although Tiktaalik's true colors are unknown to science, it likely lived in swampy areas. I glazed this figurine with a dark brown back and an orange underside to mimic the countershading present in modern animals that live in similar habitats, such as the adult Eastern Newt. 2024.

Sinosauropteryx Chasing a Small Mammal

Digital painting of Sinosauropteryx chasing a mammal based off of Zhangheotherium, a jawbone of which was found in the stomach of a fossil of Sinosauropteryx (source: https://www.app.pan.pl/article/item/app51-001.html). 2024.
